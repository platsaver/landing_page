Keyboard

There are main components in a keyboard:

  1. Case

    2. This is the component that contains all other components

  2. Plate

    3. This is the component used for holding switch straight

    This component is optional (sometime) due to the way people build.

  3. Switch

    4. This is the part that mostly contributes the sound and feel of the keyboard.

    This will be soldered into the component called PCB.

    The switch after soldered into the PCB:

  4. PCB

    5. Printed Circuit Board. This is the components that switch will be soldered into.

    Switch will create electro static into the PCB. The PCB after receiving signal, send it to the computer to display the letter

    For example, when you type P. The switch leaf will move, causing signal to the solder joint on the PCB.

    The PCB after receive that signal, bring it to the computer. Computer receive the signal and display the letter "P"

I will just put most popular mounting methods here:

  1. Top mount

    2. This mounting method performed by screwing the plate to the top frame of the case.

    Pros:

    1. Providing a solid, sturdier typing experience.
    2. Can be high pitched, which is favourable by community.

    Cons:

    1. If not executed well, the sound will be really ass
    2. Really stiff, which is not suitable to people prefering soft typing experience.
  2. Gasket mount

    3. This mounting method has it core function: Isolated plate and pcb from the case.

    It can be executed through foam strip, gummy oring, oring on the standoff, ...

    Pros:

    1. Soft and flexy typing experience.
    2. Sometimes high pitched, which many people prefer.
    3. Easier to execute than top mount, has more space for mistakes

    Cons:

    1. If not executed well, sound can be muted. This depends on people's taste, doesn't neccessarily a cons.
  3. Gummy O'ring gasket mount

    4. Same as gummy oring, which is isolated pcb,plate from case using a large oring wrapped around the gap between pcb and plate.

    Pros:

    1. Provide a moderate flexy and soft typing experience.
    2. Providing a high pitched, clacky sound.
    3. Easier to executed than 2 mounting styles that have listed.
    4. Easier , cheaper to manufacture

    Cons

    1. Depends on how good the case is manufactured.
    2. Sound at the edge of keyboard is muted than the middle part
    3. Can scratch the case if assembling uncarefully
